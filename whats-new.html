What's New

A big week for shou.be

Week of 21–27 June 2026

This week we made shou easier to explore, more accurate, and prettier to look at — plus we switched on a brand-new map view and started posting to Instagram automatically. Here's everything that changed, in plain language, grouped by area.

Map view is live Smarter search & “Surprise me” Automated Instagram posts Cleaner listings in 7 languages

1 Finding what's on

Search and recommendations that actually understand “tonight”.

  • New “Surprise me” button — one tap serves a hand-picked event for tonight, leaning toward things happening this evening and soon, with honest wording about timing.
  • Repeating events finally behave. Weekly club nights and recurring shows now turn up for any date they actually run on — not just their first date — both in search and in “Surprise me”.
  • Type an event's name, get that event. Exact-title searches now reliably surface the right result.
  • Tidier results — duplicate listings are removed, and simple “category + date” searches give consistent, sensible answers.
  • We stopped the app from claiming results were “for your dates” when you hadn't actually picked any.

2 New map view New

See what's happening around you, on a real map.

  • A List / Map toggle on the home page lets you browse events on a Google map.
  • One pin per venue, showing how many events are on there. Tap a pin to see them grouped by date and ordered by start time.
  • Fixed a handful of pins that were appearing in the wrong place (outside Belgium) due to bad location data.
  • Map pin colours now match the category legend, so it all reads consistently.

3 Cleaner, more accurate listings

Less clutter, better detail, no “zombie” events.

  • Duplicates merged. The same event listed by different sources — and ticket-tier variants like “VIP” or “Terrace” — are now folded into one clean listing.
  • Duplicate venues merged where bilingual names or town mergers had split one place into several.
  • Start and end times shown everywhere — on cards, in the pop-up, and in lists — including club-night door and closing times.
  • Recurring events now show their run dates, a weekly timetable, and the next upcoming dates.
  • Past events are hidden from browse, recommendations, and trending.
  • Always-open venues and services (not really “events”) are no longer mixed into discovery.
  • Filled in missing venue locations so more events appear on the map, and added a “real photo only” check so placeholder images don't show.

4 Your language, done right

shou speaks 7 languages — this week it got noticeably better at it.

  • Smarter title translation — descriptive words get translated while real names and brands are kept as-is.
  • Fixed events that were wrongly shown untranslated, and made the app respect each event's original language.
  • Cleared a large translation backlog and stopped wasted effort (e.g. “translating Dutch into Dutch”), so more events now appear in your language.
  • Opening a shared link in a specific language now shows the right language and selector straight away.

5 A more polished home page

Small touches that make the app feel calmer and clearer.

  • Redesigned hero with an AI-concierge feel.
  • “My events” — your liked and going lists are now one simple section, with a friendlier empty state.
  • Simpler cards & pop-ups — a single share button, one tidy action row, and a clean “venue · city · distance” line.
  • Event counts on the filter tabs and an accurate “Live” count.
  • The trust bar now reads “Updated daily” with a live “~N on tonight” figure, in a neat pill.
  • “Load more” now hides itself when there's nothing left, and we added an Instagram link in the footer.

6 Automated Instagram New

shou now promotes itself, on a weekly rhythm.

  • Posts go out automatically — feed carousels and stories on a set weekly schedule.
  • Three themes: concerts, nightlife, and now art & exhibitions (added this week — feed on Wednesdays, story on Saturdays).
  • Beautiful auto-made graphics with short AI summaries and “what to expect” highlights, matching the in-app cards.
  • Each post tags the venues and adds relevant hashtags in the first comment.
  • Fixed an issue where certain photo formats weren't showing up on the generated cards.

7 Behind the scenes

Reliability and quality work you won't see — but will feel.

  • Fixed loading errors caused by caching, and made the site more dependable across devices.
  • Shared links open the right event reliably, even at the moment of sign-in.
  • Error monitoring now runs only on the live site, keeping things clean.
  • A weekly “Top 5 exhibitions” section was added to the This Week page, grouped by city.
